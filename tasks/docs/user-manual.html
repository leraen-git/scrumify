1. Introduction

1.1 What is A.R.G.O?

A.R.G.O is an agile sprint management platform that helps your team plan, track, and improve its work using Scrum methodology. It provides a shared workspace where you can see sprint progress, monitor story completion, and track team performance over time.

Sprint Board

Visual Kanban board showing all user stories and their current status in the active sprint.

Team Dashboard

Key metrics and charts summarizing team velocity, completion rates, and category distribution.

Sprint History

Browse all past and current sprints, with detailed progress and completion data for each.

AI Insights

AI-powered retrospective analysis based on recent sprint data to help identify improvements.

1.2 Your Role as a User

As a regular user, you have read-only access to your team's workspace. This means you can view all information, charts, and sprint boards — but creating, editing, or deleting content is handled by your Scrum Master or Admin.

You CanAdmin Only
View the active sprint boardAdd, edit, or delete stories
Browse all sprints and their detailsCreate or manage sprints
View team metrics and velocity chartsMove stories between statuses
Run AI retrospective analysisManage developers and settings
View team roster and capacityAdd or remove team members
Questions about your team's content? Contact your Scrum Master or Admin if you need stories added, sprint dates changed, or other modifications made to your workspace.

2. Accessing A.R.G.O

2.1 Using Your Access Link

You do not need a password to access A.R.G.O. Your Admin will provide you with a unique access link — a URL that logs you in automatically and takes you directly to your team workspace.

1
Receive your access link from your Scrum Master or Admin (usually shared by email or messaging).
2
Click the link or paste it into your browser's address bar.
3
You are automatically logged in and redirected to your team's Dashboard.
Tip: Bookmark your team dashboard URL after your first login so you can return directly next time without needing the access link.
Note: Your access link is unique to you. Do not share it with others, as it grants access to your team's workspace under your name. If you think your link has been compromised, ask your Admin to generate a new one.

2.2 Navigating the Interface

After logging in, you will see the A.R.G.O navigation bar at the top of every page.

ElementDescription
A.R.G.O logoClick to return to your team Dashboard
Logout buttonSigns you out and clears your session

Team Workspace Tabs

Your team workspace has one tab visible to you: Dashboard. From the Dashboard, you can navigate to all sections of your team's workspace including Sprints and Velocity.

Note: Some tabs (Team, Settings) are visible only to Administrators. Your view may differ from what your Admin sees.

3. Team Dashboard

The Team Dashboard is the first page you see after logging in. It provides a complete overview of your team's current and historical performance.

3.1 Overview Metrics

Five metric cards at the top of the dashboard summarize your team's performance:

MetricWhat It Means
Total SprintsTotal number of sprints your team has run
Average VelocityAverage story points delivered per completed sprint
Avg Dev TimeAverage time a story spends being developed (In Progress status)
Avg Test TimeAverage time a story spends in testing (Dev Done status)
Avg Completion RateOn average, what percentage of planned capacity your team delivers

3.2 Velocity & Category Charts

Two charts help you visualize your team's performance over time:

Velocity Chart

A bar and line chart comparing SP Planned (what was planned) vs. SP Done (what was delivered) for each sprint. A dashed line shows your average velocity. Use the sprint toggle buttons above the chart to show or hide specific sprints.

Category Donut Chart

A pie chart showing how your team's story points are distributed across work types: User Story, Bug, MCO (operational tasks), Best-effort, and Tech Lead. This helps understand your team's workload balance.

Story Card Information

Each story card shows:

  • Story ID — a short identifier (first 8 characters)
  • Story title — description of the work item
  • Story points — estimated complexity/effort
  • Assignee — the developer assigned to the story (if any)
  • Category badge — type of work (User Story, Bug, MCO, etc.)
  • Dev time badge dev Xh — total time the story spent in development. Visible on stories that have moved past In Progress, including completed stories.
  • Test time badge test Xh — total time the story spent in testing. Visible on stories that have moved past Dev Done, including completed stories.
Tip: If a story's point value appears in red, it means the estimate was changed at some point. Hover over the number to see the full history of point changes.
Note: Dev and test times accumulate correctly even if a story is sent back to development after testing. The total reflects all time spent in each status combined.

3.4 Upcoming & Completed Sprints

Below the active sprint board, the dashboard shows sprint cards in two sections:

Upcoming Sprints

Planned sprints that have not yet started. Each card shows:

  • Sprint name and date range
  • Planned status badge
  • Story points currently planned

Completed Sprints

Finished sprints. Each card shows:

  • Sprint name and date range
  • Story points delivered out of capacity
  • Progress bar showing completion percentage

Click any sprint card to open its full details page.

3.5 Project Forecast

Below the sprint cards, the dashboard shows a Project Forecast chart that visualises past performance and predicts how many future sprints will be needed to complete all backlog work.

Reading the Chart

Bar colourWhat it represents
Indigo (light)Story points planned in a past sprint
Indigo (dark)Story points actually delivered in a past sprint
AmberActive sprint (planned and in-progress delivery)
GreyFuture sprint capacity (forecast)

Sliding Window

The chart displays 7 sprints at a time on a wide screen (5 on a narrow screen), centred on the active sprint. Use the ← → arrows to scroll left or right through all sprints.

Overflow Sprints

If the backlog contains more work than can fit in the currently planned sprints, the chart automatically generates forecast sprints (labelled "Sprint N — forecast"). These are synthetic future sprints that show how many additional sprints would be needed to clear the backlog at the team's current velocity.

Summary Badges

At the top-right of the forecast panel you will see:

  • Backlog — number of stories and total story points waiting to be planned
  • N sprints ahead — total future sprints (planned + overflow) required to deliver all backlog work

4. Sprints

4.1 Viewing All Sprints

Navigate to the Sprint section from the Dashboard's sprint cards or by navigating to your team's sprint list. This page shows all sprints organized by status.

Active & Planned Sprints

Current and upcoming sprints are shown at the top. Each card displays sprint name, dates, capacity, story point progress, and time elapsed.

Completed Sprints

Past sprints are grouped in a collapsible section at the bottom. Click the Sprints Completed (N) label to expand and see them.

Sprint Status Badges

  • Active — sprint is currently in progress
  • Planned — sprint is scheduled but not yet started
  • Completed — sprint has ended

4.2 Sprint Board Details

Click any sprint card to open its detailed board view.

Sprint Statistics

Five cards at the top of the sprint board show real-time statistics:

StatisticWhat It Shows
SP CapacityTotal story points the team can deliver in this sprint
SP PlannedStory points the team aimed to deliver
SP CurrentTotal story points across all stories in the sprint
SP DoneStory points of completed stories
Completion %Percentage of capacity delivered

Progress Bars

Two progress bars provide a visual summary:

  • Story Points progress — SP Done vs. SP Capacity. Green ≥80%, amber 50–79%, red <50%.
  • Time Elapsed — working days used vs. remaining. Red if >80% of time is used (urgency indicator), amber if 50–79%, green if <50%.
Tip: The progress bars use working days (Monday–Friday), not calendar days. Weekends are not counted.

Category Breakdown

A table shows how stories are distributed across categories for this sprint, including SP done vs. total per category with color-coded completion percentages.

Transition Times

Two time metrics appear below the category breakdown:

  • Avg In Progress → Dev Done — average time your team spends developing stories
  • Avg Testing → Done — average time your team spends testing stories

These metrics help identify bottlenecks in the development process.

The Kanban Board

The full Kanban board shows all sprint stories organized into four columns:

ColumnWhat It Means
○ To DoWork that hasn't been started yet
⏱ In ProgressA developer is actively working on this
⚗ Dev Done / TestingDevelopment complete, awaiting testing or review
✓ DoneFully completed and accepted

Reading Story Cards

In the detailed sprint board, story cards contain additional information compared to the dashboard view:

  • Story ID — unique short identifier
  • Title — story description
  • Category badge — type of work with custom color
  • Story points — shown in red if ever modified (hover for history)
  • Assignee — developer assigned to the story
  • Dev time badge dev Xh — accumulated time in development. Shown on In Progress, Dev Done, and Done stories.
  • Test time badge test Xh — accumulated time in testing. Shown on Dev Done and Done stories.
Note: On the detailed sprint board, you will not see edit or move buttons — those are available to Admins only. Your view is read-only, showing all the same story information without modification controls.
Tip: Dev and test times accumulate even if a story is moved back-and-forth between statuses. The badge always shows the total combined time across all transitions.

5. Velocity

The Velocity page provides in-depth analytics about your team's historical performance. Access it by navigating to your team and clicking the Velocity link from the Sprint section.

5.1 Velocity Chart

The velocity chart shows your team's performance over completed sprints:

  • Blue bars — story points planned for each sprint
  • Green bars — story points actually delivered
  • Blue line — velocity per developer-day (efficiency metric)
  • Dashed line — your team's average velocity across all sprints

Use the sprint toggle buttons above the chart to filter which sprints are included in the view. This is useful for excluding outlier sprints or focusing on recent performance.

Category Distribution Chart

A donut chart below the velocity chart shows the percentage breakdown of story types across all selected sprints. This helps you understand whether your team is spending more time on new features, bugs, operational work, or other categories.

5.2 AI Retrospective Advisor

A.R.G.O includes an AI-powered retrospective tool that analyzes your team's recent sprint data and provides actionable recommendations.

1
On the Velocity page, find the AI Retrospective Advisor section (marked with a "Powered by Claude" badge).
2
Click Analyse to start the analysis. This may take a few seconds.
3
The AI will generate a formatted analysis with insights about your team's performance trends and recommendations.
4
Click Regenerate to run a fresh analysis at any time.
Tip: The AI advisor analyzes the last 3 completed sprints. For the most useful insights, ensure your team has at least 2–3 completed sprints with accurate story point data.

Sprint Performance Table

At the bottom of the Velocity page, a table lists all sprints with:

  • Sprint name and date range
  • Capacity — team story point capacity for that sprint
  • Planned — story points targeted
  • Delivered — actual story points completed
  • Efficiency — delivered ÷ capacity (color-coded: green ≥80%, amber 50–79%, red <50%)

6. Team Members

The Team tab shows your team's composition, capacity, and availability. As a user, this is a read-only view.

6.1 Team Statistics

Three metric cards at the top of the Team page provide capacity information:

MetricDescription
Days worked this sprintTotal working days × number of developers since the current sprint started
Total days workedTotal working days × number of developers since the team's first sprint
Theoretical capacitySum of all developer story point capacities per sprint

6.2 Days Off Calendar

The calendar shows planned days off for all developers in the team. Each row represents one developer, and each column represents a day of the month.

  • ½ in a cell — the developer has a half-day off that day
  • ✕ in a cell — the developer has a full day off that day
  • Empty — the developer is working that day
  • Gray columns — weekends (Saturday and Sunday)

Use the Previous / Next navigation arrows to browse different months.

Note: As a regular user, you can view the calendar but cannot add or remove days off. Contact your Admin to update day off records.

6.3 Developer Roster

Below the calendar, the developer list shows each team member with:

  • Name with avatar initial
  • Role — Developer or Tech Lead
  • Story point capacity — how many SP they can deliver per full sprint
Tip: The sum of all developers' story point capacities equals the team's Theoretical Capacity shown in the cards above. This is the maximum SP the team could deliver in a full, uninterrupted sprint.

6.4 Logging Out

To log out of A.R.G.O, click the Logout button in the top navigation bar. Your session will be cleared and you will be redirected to the login page.

To access A.R.G.O again, use your access link provided by your Admin. If you have bookmarked your team dashboard, you may need to use the access link again after logging out.

Need help? If you experience any issues accessing your workspace, cannot find your access link, or have questions about your team's sprint data, please contact your Scrum Master or A.R.G.O Administrator.
